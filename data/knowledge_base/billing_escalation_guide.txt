BILLING ESCALATION GUIDE

Effective Date: January 2024

1. WHEN TO ESCALATE A BILLING TICKET

A billing ticket must be escalated immediately if any of the following conditions apply:

- The customer reports payment was successfully charged but service was not activated.
- The customer reports duplicate charges for the same transaction.
- A refund has been pending for more than 48 hours beyond the expected processing time.
- The customer expresses significant frustration, requests a supervisor, or threatens to cancel service due to a billing issue.
- The total disputed amount exceeds $500.
- The Support Team cannot resolve the billing issue using standard procedures.

2. HOW TO ESCALATE

When a billing ticket meets escalation criteria:

- The ticket must be marked as High Priority in the ticketing system.
- The Billing Team must be notified through the designated Billing Escalations channel.
- The customer must receive an acknowledgment within 1 hour.

The following information must be included in the escalation:

- Customer Account ID.
- Customer email address.
- Transaction ID, if available.
- Amount in dispute.
- Description of the issue.
- Actions already taken by the Support Team.
- Any supporting screenshots or payment receipts.

3. ESCALATION RESPONSE TIMES

The following response targets apply:

- High Priority Escalations: within 2 hours.
- Medium Priority Escalations: within 24 hours.

A billing issue is considered Medium Priority when:
- The disputed amount is $500 or less.
- Service remains active.
- No duplicate charge or failed activation is involved.

All escalated billing tickets should be resolved within 3 business days whenever possible.

If additional investigation is required, the customer must be informed of the revised timeline.

4. CUSTOMER COMMUNICATION DURING ESCALATION

Customers must be informed throughout the escalation process.

Required communications include:

- An acknowledgment within 1 hour of escalation.
- A status update every 24 hours until resolution.
- A resolution email when the issue is closed.

Each update should include:
- Current investigation status.
- Actions completed since the previous update.
- Expected next steps, if available.

5. ESCALATION OUTCOMES

After an escalated billing ticket is resolved, the following must be documented:

- Root cause of the issue.
- Steps taken to resolve the issue.
- Any refund, credit, or billing adjustment provided.
- Process improvements identified to prevent recurrence.

This information must be recorded in the audit log and retained for future reference.
