ACCOUNT ESCALATION GUIDE

Effective Date: January 2024

1. WHEN TO ESCALATE AN ACCOUNT TICKET

An account ticket must be escalated immediately if any of the following conditions apply:

- The customer reports suspected unauthorized access to their account.
- The customer is unable to access their account for more than 24 hours.
- The customer reports missing account data or potential data loss.
- The customer requests account cancellation and expresses significant frustration, anger, or dissatisfaction.
- The customer is on an Enterprise Plan and reports any account-related issue.
- The Support Team is unable to resolve the issue after standard troubleshooting steps have been completed.

2. SECURITY RELATED ESCALATIONS

Any ticket involving a suspected security incident must be escalated to the Security Team within 30 minutes.

Examples of security incidents include:
- Unauthorized login attempts.
- Account takeover concerns.
- Suspicious password changes.
- Unrecognized account activity.
- Suspected security breaches.

Before escalation:
- Advise the customer to change their password immediately.
- Verify the customer's identity using standard account verification procedures.
- Flag the account for Security Review.

All security-related escalations are classified as High Priority regardless of the customer's stated urgency.

3. ESCALATION RESPONSE TIMES

The following acknowledgment targets apply:

- Security Escalations: within 30 minutes.
- Enterprise Account Escalations: within 1 hour.
- General Account Escalations: within 2 hours.

Response time targets apply 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

4. INFORMATION REQUIRED FOR ESCALATION

The following information must be included in every escalation request:

- Customer Account ID.
- Customer email address.
- Description of the issue.
- Date and time the issue was reported.
- Troubleshooting steps already performed.
- Whether a security concern is involved.
- Whether the customer is on an Enterprise Plan.
- Any screenshots, logs, or supporting evidence provided by the customer.

Incomplete escalation requests may be returned for additional information.

5. CUSTOMER COMMUNICATION DURING ESCALATION

Customers must receive an acknowledgment within 1 hour after an escalation is submitted.

For Security Escalations:
- Customers must receive status updates every 2 hours until the issue is resolved or contained.

For General and Enterprise Escalations:
- Customers must receive status updates at least once every business day until resolution.

All customer communications related to escalations must be recorded in the audit trail, including:
- Date and time of communication.
- Communication channel used.
- Summary of information provided.
