SHIPPING DELAY POLICY

Effective Date: January 2024

1. WHAT CONSTITUTES A SHIPPING DELAY
A shipment is considered delayed when it has not been delivered within 3 business days of the estimated delivery date. Customers must be proactively notified of any known delays before the estimated delivery date passes. If a delay is identified after the estimated delivery date the customer must be notified within 24 hours.

2. CAUSES OF SHIPPING DELAYS
Shipping delays can occur due to high order volumes during peak seasons, severe weather conditions, customs clearance for international orders, courier operational issues, or incorrect delivery address provided by the customer. The cause of the delay must be identified and communicated to the customer clearly.

3. CUSTOMER COMMUNICATION DURING DELAYS
When a delay is identified the customer must receive an updated estimated delivery date within 24 hours. If the updated estimated delivery date changes again the customer must be notified immediately. Customers must never be left without an update for more than 48 hours during an active delay.

4. COMPENSATION FOR DELAYS
Customers whose orders are delayed by more than 7 business days beyond the original estimated delivery date are eligible for a shipping fee refund. Customers whose orders are delayed by more than 14 business days are eligible for a full refund or a replacement shipment at no additional cost.

5. ESCALATION OF DELAY CASES
Shipping delay cases must be escalated to the shipping team when the delay exceeds 7 business days, when the customer expresses frustration or urgency, or when the tracking information has not been updated for more than 48 hours. All escalated delay cases must be resolved within 3 business days of escalation.
